FRANK & FRAN'S HATTERAS ISLAND FISHING REPORT - 7/23/2026


Today was a litte sun, little rain, LOTS of RAIN, more sun. Everyone tried to fish between the bad weather and those that did found some sea mullet, pompano and croaker. Tomorrow looks like we are in for some more rain. Good day to grab some rain gear and keep on fishing!

DOUBLED UP ON THE SOUND SIDE - Courtney sent us this photo of the guys doubled up on the soundside last night on cut mullet. Way to go guys!

FIRST DRUM - Tim sent us a picture of his son with his first catch. A nice 30" drum is a great way to get hooked! Congratulations!!!

We also had some special visitors in the shop today. David Deuel's family stopped in to see the world record drum on display at the shop. On November 7, 1984, David Deuel set the IGFA All-Tackle and Men's 50lb Line Class World Record with this the massive red drum in this photo. He was surf fishing from the beach in Avon, North Carolina, when the 94-pound, 2-ounce record fish struck the cut mullet he was using for bait. He brought the fish to the shop and Fran, one of the orginal owners was the official weighmaster. His family came down to see the drum and the rod and reel he used to catch it. It was a very cool visite and we appreciate them stopping in!
