FRANK & FRAN'S HATTERAS ISLAND FISHING REPORT - 4/20/26


Hatteras Island Fishing Report — April 20, 2026

Spring fishing is in full swing across Hatteras Island today with partly sunny skies, a high near 67°F, and southwest winds around 10–15 mph keeping the surf lively. The ocean temperature sits at 66°F, and the UV index is moderate — a perfect day for anglers chasing drum and blues along the beaches.

In Avon, the Mulligan family from Vermont had a great day on the sand, landing 11 fish total — 4 bluefish, 6 drum, and 1 ray that broke off right at the beach. Only one drum was a keeper, but the steady action made for an exciting outing.

Beth checked in from Ramp 25 this morning, reporting a spot and a whiting this afternoon — still on the hunt for those drum!

Patrick hooked into another huge bluefish, continuing the strong blue bite we’ve seen this week.
And Elaine shared, “Blues and one red drum this a.m. — all catch and release!”

The surf bite remains steady, with blues hitting cut bait and spoons, and drum preferring shrimp and mullet chunks on bottom rigs. As water temps climb, expect more consistent action for sea mullet, blues, and drum through the week. The Rodanthe pier has also reported Grey Trout! The Sound side action also seems to be starting to kick off with some amazing Blues being caught on mullet!

Several folks have messaged or called lately just to say how much they appreciate our kindness, friendly advice, and help getting on the fish — and we say, that’s what you get when you fish with Frank & Fran’s! #hookedonfandf

Tide tip: The incoming morning tide continues to produce the best results for drum and blues.
Local note: Avon Pier and the beaches near Ramps 25–43 are seeing the most consistent bites.
