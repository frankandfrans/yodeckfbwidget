FRANK AND FRANS FISHING REPORT FOR 6/13/25

It has been a pretty peaceful Friday the 13th, no sightings of Jason. The weather has been hot, humid, and a few scattered raindrops. The northern beaches have seen the ever-present pinhead croaker, a few sea mullet, and bluefish. Nighttime, there have been a decent number of spinner and blacktip sharks. Avon Pier has had blues, croakers, and sharks. Ramp 49, yet again, croaker, some better sea mullet, and blues. Ramp 55 has had a good showing of pompano and a decent sea mullet bite. The sound side has been producing blues and trout. Nighttime in the sound is producing bigger drum and bigger blues. Ryan and Ollie were busy catching baby blacktips on Avon Pier. Marco caught a fairly rare Banded drum recently, don't see those very often. He also caught a sea mullet. His sister Gracie caught a nice Spot. Curtis caught himself a nice 2# 15oz citation Pompano, bet you can't guess on what rig. Rick of Avon caught a nice blue on his boat in the sound.
